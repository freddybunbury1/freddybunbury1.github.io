Freddy Bunbury

I study how microbial traits and interactions determine ecological performance under environmental change. My work integrates two complementary axes: intrinsic responses to abiotic stress, with a focus on temperature, and interaction-mediated responses through shared metabolites, with a focus on vitamin B12.

  • Measuring microbial isolate traits at scale
  • Predicting traits from genomes and identifying associated genes
  • Modeling microbial intrinsic and interaction traits to predict performance in communities

Research

Interaction traits: vitamin B12 exchange
I quantify B12 synthesis, release, and uptake traits across bacterial isolates to understand how metabolites are shared and how this shapes community structure.

Intrinsic traits: thermotolerance
I study how cyanobacteria adapt to high temperatures in hot springs, linking genomic features to growth and environmental distributions.
